About This Book

The Great Gatsby is a 1925 novel by American writer F. Scott Fitzgerald. Set in the Jazz Age on prosperous Long Island and in New York City during the summer of 1922, the novel follows the tragic story of Jay Gatsby as he attempts to win back his former lover Daisy Buchanan. The story is narrated by Nick Carraway, Daisy's cousin who becomes Gatsby's neighbor.

Widely regarded as one of the greatest American novels, The Great Gatsby explores themes of decadence, idealism, resistance to change, social upheaval, and excess, creating a portrait of the Roaring Twenties that has been described as a cautionary tale regarding the American Dream.
